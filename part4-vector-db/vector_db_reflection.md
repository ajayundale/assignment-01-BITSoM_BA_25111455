## Vector DB Use Case

### From Keywords to Context

The traditional approach to document retrieval—**keyword-based search**—often falls short in the legal domain. Law firms manage large, dense contracts where key clauses can be expressed using a wide range of terminology. For instance, a termination clause might appear as “cessation of agreement,” “contract dissolution,” or “end of service.” As keyword search relies on literal string matching, a lawyer must guess the exact phrasing used by the original author. This not only reduces efficiency but also increases the risk of missing critical information.

**Vector databases** solve this by shifting the focus from *"what words were used"* to ***"what was meant."***

By representing both the lawyer’s query and the contract text as ***mathematical embeddings***, the system performs a ***semantic search***. This allows a lawyer to ask a question in plain English—*"How can we end this partnership?"*—and retrieve relevant sections even if the document never uses the word *"end"*. Lawyers can now interact with documents in natural language without needing to anticipate exact phrasing.

Additionally, vector databases are well-suited for handling large volumes of text, such as lengthy contracts spanning hundreds of pages. They enable advanced capabilities like contextual ranking and clustering. In this context, the vector database serves as the foundation for intelligent document search, effectively bridging the gap between human language and complex legal text.

Ultimately, while keyword search acts as a simple index, a vector database acts as **an intelligent backbone**, ensuring that legal discovery is precise, efficient, and context-aware.
